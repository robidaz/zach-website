
# Books

These are books that I have read and found to be particularly insightful or influential in my work. They cover a range of topics including software development, design, and project management.

- Freakonomics: A Rogue Economist Explores the Hidden Side of Everything" by Steven D. Levitt and Stephen J. Dubner
- The Singularity Is Near: When Humans Transcend Biology" by Ray Kurzweil
- The Singularity is Nearer: A True Story About the Future" by Ray Kurzweil
- Nexus: A brief history of information Networks from the Stone Age to AI: Yuval Noah Harari
- The Master Algorithm: How the Quest for the Ultimate Learning Machine Will Remake Our World" by Pedro Domingos
- The First Time Manager: How to Manage People When You’re New to Being a Boss" by Loren B. Belker, Jim McCormick, and Gary S. Topchik
- Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind by Yuval Noah Harari
- Homo Deus: A Brief History of Tomorrow by Yuval Noah Harari
- Smart Brevity: The Power of Saying More with Less" by Jim VandeHei, Mike Allen, and Roy Schwartz
- Algospeak: How Social media is transforming the future of language by Adam Aleksik
- Scary Smart: The Future of Artificial Intelligence and How You Can Save Our World by Mo Gawdat
- The Five Dysfunctions of a Team by Patrick Lencioni
- When Everyone knows that Everyone knows: Common Knowledge and the Mysteries of Money, Power and Everyday Life by Steven Pinker
- Conscious: A Brief Guide to the Fundamental Mystery of the Mind by Annaka Harris
- Discrimination and Disparities: by Thomas Sowell
